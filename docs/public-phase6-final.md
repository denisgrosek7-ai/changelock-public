# Phase 6 Market Verifiability Final

This document closes Phase 6 as the bounded public-proof and third-party-verifiability layer above the existing execution, intelligence, enterprise, and production-usability foundation.

## Covered by Phase 6

- bounded transparency anchoring over verifier-facing public artifacts
- public proof portal with scope-aware proof views and freshness semantics
- trust badge verification linked to underlying proof freshness
- reproducible benchmark pack metadata with methodology and customer-run replay guidance
- reference architecture conformance with visible matched, degraded, deviated, and unsupported states
- independent verifier SDK completion with compatibility and deprecation discipline
- bounded auditor workflow with permissioned export refs
- claims governance with public, partner, and auditor scope separation
- final `phase6_market_verifiability_active` gate

## Final state model

- `phase6_incomplete`
- `phase6_substantially_ready`
- `phase6_market_verifiability_active`

## Boundaries

- no new truth store is introduced; all Phase 6 surfaces remain projections over the published evidence spine
- public proof does not expose customer-sensitive runtime, incident, workflow, or unpublished partner data
- trust badges remain bounded freshness and verifier-availability signals, not universal security guarantees
- benchmark packs remain methodology-bound publication artifacts, not blanket market-performance claims
- conformance surfaces keep unsupported and deviated states visible instead of collapsing them into a single score
- verifier outputs remain verifier results and do not replace local admissibility, legal review, or customer policy
- auditor workflows remain bounded to public or auditor-safe artifacts

## Not claimed by Phase 6

- formal certification or regulator approval
- universal interoperability across unpublished schema lines
- global trust authority or vendor-only verification centralization
- market authority beyond published, freshness-bound, verifier-friendly evidence
