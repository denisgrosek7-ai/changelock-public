# Phase 8 Formalized Authority Plan

This document is a locked planning document for a future `Phase 8`.

It is intentionally strict:

- it describes intended scope, controls, and review boundaries
- it does not claim current implementation
- it does not override the documentation truth policy
- it exists to keep `Phase 8` reviewable, legally bounded, and resistant to interpretive drift

Before treating any `Phase 8` statement as implementation status, read [documentation-truth-policy.md](/Users/denisgrosek/.codex/worktrees/5553/changelock-blueprint/docs/documentation-truth-policy.md).

## 0. Purpose

`Phase 8` is the transition from:

- verifier-friendly market proof
- ecosystem presence
- enterprise workflow ownership

to:

- a formally acceptable, institutionally usable, and strictly governed authority-support layer

This is the phase where ChangeLock is intended to become usable inside:

- insurer review processes
- regulator-facing controls
- certification support workflows
- formal compliance programs
- tightly governed trust and authority processes

The goal is:

turn technically verifiable truth into a formally acceptable and institutionally usable trust framework without claiming that the platform itself becomes law, regulator, insurer, or certification authority.

## 1. Foundational Rule

`Phase 8` must not confuse formal acceptability with self-declared authority.

That means:

1. ChangeLock does not become law; it provides machine-executable and evidence-traceable support.
2. Insurance, regulator, and certification outputs must remain bounded, explainable, versioned, and audited.
3. No AI or consensus layer becomes authoritative without explicit human or formal governance boundaries.
4. Formal surfaces never overwrite the canonical evidence spine.
5. Institutional authority is built on verifiable process, not branding posture.
6. Every formal output must declare:
   - legal weight
   - methodological status
   - allowed use
   - interpretation limits

This remains aligned with the core rule:

one truth, many projections.

## 2. Phase 8 Entry Gate

`Phase 8` must not open only because `Phase 7` is complete.

Before work starts, the entry gate must confirm:

- `Phase 7` is closed as a bounded ecosystem phase
- `Phase 7` deferred scope remains explicitly deferred
- claim and publication governance from `Phase 6` and `Phase 7` remains consistent
- a canonical formal-authority baseline doc exists
- there are no open correctness blockers in evidence custody, export boundaries, or deferred-scope discipline
- there is explicit separation between:
  - `core pass`
  - `institutional expansion`
  - out-of-scope items

The purpose is to prevent:

- premature authority tone
- silent drift into legal or regulatory overclaim
- mixing institutional ambition with actual formal readiness

## 3. Phase 8 Goal

At completion, `Phase 8` must add three capabilities:

### 3.1 Formal Risk and Compliance Interface

ChangeLock must be able to express proofs and controls in forms usable by:

- insurers
- regulators
- certification bodies
- auditors
- formal governance boards

### 3.2 Codified Compliance Execution

ChangeLock must support:

- formalized compliance profiles
- regulatory control mappings
- assessor-ready evidence packs
- machine-checkable compliance posture
- versioned and jurisdiction-aware compliance outputs

### 3.3 Governed Autonomy

ChangeLock must support:

- federated or consensus-assisted review
- formal review
- bounded policy evolution
- AI-assisted governance

without becoming an uncontrolled AI tribunal.

## 6. Formal Claim Taxonomy

Every formal output must have a claim class.

Required classes:

- `supporting_evidence_only`
- `machine_checkable_control_evidence`
- `assessor_facing_summary`
- `insurer_facing_risk_input`
- `regulator_safe_disclosure`
- `certification_support_artifact`
- `not_valid_as_formal_claim`

Required additional fields:

- `allowed_use`
- `prohibited_use`
- `standard_of_proof`
- `audience_class`
- `human_review_requirement`

No output may be released without:

- claim class
- scope
- methodology
- owner
- freshness window
- usage limits
- revocation or supersession status
- allowed and prohibited use discipline

## 7. Formal Use-Permission Matrix

Every formal output must map:

- audience
- claim class
- allowed use
- forbidden use
- sharing scope
- redaction profile
- whether human approval is required
- whether quorum is required
- whether export is allowed
- whether public citation is allowed

This exists because correct outputs are often misused after release, not only built incorrectly.

## 8. Standard-of-Proof Model

Every formal output must declare:

- required evidence sufficiency floor
- minimum review level
- whether human attestation is required
- whether independent second-party review is required
- whether external assessor confirmation is required

Suggested proof classes:

- `technical_support_only`
- `formal_internal_reviewed`
- `assessor_review_ready`
- `external_reliance_ready`
- `not_sufficient_for_external_reliance`

The purpose is to prevent outputs with different evidentiary weight from appearing equivalent.

## 9. Jurisdiction, Effective-Date, and Deprecation Model

The model must include:

- `conflicts_with`
- `jurisdiction_priority_rule`
- `fallback_interpretation_mode`

This is required because multi-jurisdiction handling needs more than simple applicability; it also needs conflict behavior and fallback semantics.

## 10. Conflict-of-Law and Multi-Jurisdiction Resolution

The system must support:

- multiple profiles on the same subject
- conflicting applicability rules
- conflicting effective-date boundaries
- regional override
- organization-specific stricter overlays
- priority ordering
- `cannot_resolve_automatically` outcomes

Required states:

- `single_profile_applicable`
- `multiple_profiles_compatible`
- `multiple_profiles_conflicting`
- `manual_resolution_required`
- `out_of_jurisdiction_scope`

## 11. Evidence Custody, Legal Hold, and Redaction Chain

Formal artifacts must not be released without:

- export ID
- signing status
- timestamp
- custody owner
- redaction policy
- retention class
- legal-hold mode
- disclosure audience
- integrity hash or reference
- supersession or revocation record
- release approval record

Additional records should include:

- withdrawal record
- access log digest
- disclosure reason code

## 12. Formal Artifact Release and Withdrawal Workflow

Every formal artifact must have lifecycle states:

- `draft`
- `internal_review`
- `approved_for_release`
- `released`
- `challenged`
- `superseded`
- `withdrawn`
- `revoked`

Formal outputs are not just created or not created. Their lifecycle must remain operationally and legally traceable.

## 13. Challenge, Rebuttal, and Override Workflow

The workflow must include:

- response SLA
- mandatory reviewer class
- freeze-on-challenge behavior
- interim validity state

Required states:

- `challenged_pending_review`
- `rebuttal_submitted`
- `override_recorded`
- `superseded_after_review`
- `challenge_rejected`
- `withdrawn_pending_reissue`

## 14. Non-Delegable Authority Actions

The enforced register must include at least:

- approval of regulator-facing disclosure
- approval of insurer-facing external export
- approval of certification-support release
- release from legal hold
- reactivation of challenged artifact back to active status
- approval of external disclosure audience
- approval of methodology change for formal profiles

This must be enforced, not merely documented.

## 15. Separation of Duties and Quorum Discipline

For governed institutional actions, the system must define:

- who proposes
- who reviews
- who approves
- who may not be the same person as proposer
- when a two-person rule is required
- when cross-functional quorum is required
- when external assessor confirmation is required

This is especially required for:

- regulator-facing disclosure
- certification evidence-pack approval
- redaction policy change
- policy-as-law profile activation
- release from legal hold
- insurer-facing risk input export

## 16. Risk Model Calibration Discipline

The risk model layer must include:

- training or reference dataset lineage
- calibration population scope
- revalidation triggers
- adverse-use warning
- sensitivity disclosure

Risk input may support insurers or governance processes, but it must not present itself as definitive economic or legal judgment.

## 17. Model Risk Management Discipline

Every model in `Phase 8` must have:

- model ID
- owner
- intended use
- forbidden use
- training or reference basis
- drift monitoring
- bias and failure-mode notes
- rollback path
- challenger-model review where appropriate

This prevents `Phase 8` from being formally strict only at the surface while remaining internally informal.

## 18. Evidence Sufficiency Classes

Required evidence classes must support at least:

- baseline sufficiency classes
- `externally_verified`
- `contradicted`
- `superseded_evidence`

If not modeled as separate classes, they must exist as explicit evidence states.

## 19. Institutional Redaction Policy Engine

The redaction layer must include:

- disclosure basis
- legal basis where relevant
- minimum-necessary rule
- field-level rationale
- challengeable redaction decision record

This is especially important for regulator-safe and insurer-facing surfaces.

## 20. Institutional Dependency Registry

The registry must track:

- applicable laws, frameworks, and profiles
- external standards
- certification schemes
- regulator interpretation notes
- benchmark methodologies
- insurer methodology overlays

This keeps formal outputs anchored to explicit external dependencies and makes dependency change reviewable.

## 27. Prohibited Outcomes

`Phase 8` is prohibited from:

1. presenting ChangeLock as a legislator or direct legal authority
2. issuing legal verdicts where only evidence outputs are valid
3. creating an AI tribunal without bounded human or formal governance
4. issuing insurance or regulatory outputs without methodology, scope, and claim discipline
5. publishing actuarial benchmarks without privacy protection and aggregation
6. claiming machine enforceability where policy-as-law profiles cannot support it
7. treating consensus signals as fully approved truth
8. allowing cross-organization sharing without weighting and review discipline
9. creating a second formal truth base outside the evidence spine
10. using authority language where only bounded support exists
11. releasing formal exports without custody, timestamp, and redaction discipline
12. allowing AI or consensus layers to perform non-delegable authority actions
13. producing adverse institutional outputs without explanation path
14. activating formal profiles without jurisdiction and effective-date discipline
15. returning challenged artifacts to active status without formal re-review

## 28. Pass and Fail Conditions

### Phase 8 Pass

`Phase 8` is a pass when ChangeLock is:

- formally usable for insurers, regulators, and certifiers
- able to support machine-executable compliance proof
- able to support bounded consensus-assisted governance
- governed by evidence, claim taxonomy, proof standards, custody, and authority-routing rules rather than inflated authority claims

### Phase 8 Fail

`Phase 8` is a fail if any of the following occur:

- the system claims legal authority it does not have
- AI or consensus becomes authoritative too early
- insurance or regulatory outputs are not methodologically bounded
- regulator-safe surfaces leak too much information
- compliance codification claims more than the system can machine-prove
- institutional boundary discipline is missing
- formal outputs are not traceable to evidence
- custody, redaction, or legal-hold discipline is missing
- challenge or rebuttal path does not exist
- non-delegable authority actions are not protected
- compliance profiles lack jurisdiction or effective-date discipline
- outputs lack claim class or sufficiency class
- outputs lack use-permission discipline
- risk or AI models lack governance and revalidation discipline

`Phase 8` must add three non-negotiable outcomes:

1. formal risk and compliance usefulness
2. codified execution of compliance
3. governed autonomy

To make that safe, it must also add:

4. formal claim discipline
5. use-permission and standard-of-proof discipline
6. jurisdiction, version, and conflict discipline
7. evidence custody and redaction discipline
8. challenge, rebuttal, and override discipline
9. non-delegable authority discipline
10. risk-model and model-governance discipline
11. evidence sufficiency discipline
12. separation-of-duties and quorum discipline

This is the correct shape of `Phase 8`:

- not an AI tribunal phase
- not a legal monopoly phase
- not an ultimate-authority marketing layer

It is the formalization of a bounded trust platform for institutional use, built on the same evidence spine as every earlier phase.
